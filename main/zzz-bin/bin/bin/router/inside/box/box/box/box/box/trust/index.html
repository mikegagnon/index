3.3 You can’t trust me

You will notice that in Chapter 1, I attempted to persuade you
of the superior beauty of the Tau (hopefully convincingly).
Then, in Chapter 2, I contradicted myself by saying the Tau
is wrong (which although satirical, consists entirely of valid
points). In adgeneral, I would say it would probably be a
mistake to blindly trust me—to take my words at face value.

I deliberately lie to you, along with the other Tauists,
because I hold the strange notion that lying to you might
help you.
